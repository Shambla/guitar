Bio

Mr. Streckfus has been a guitar teacher for over a decade at Bel Air Music Studios as well as working at other music stores. Mr. Streckfus has over 150 publications with Hal Leonard and prides himself on his editing skills (See below for examples). Mr. Streckfus has a Bachelors in guitar performance from Peabody Conservatory of the Johns Hopkins University. Mr. Streckfus also has a Masters degree from Towson University in guitar performance in which he received an assistantship and scholarship awards, allowing him to gain experience lecturing, organizing lesson plans, and grading at a collegiate level. His former teachers include Ray Chester, who was the head chairman of guitar at Peabody Conservatory and one of the world's leading guitar pedagogues. Brian has also received music instruction from other well-trained guitarists in the Maryland region such as Peabody Alumni, James Hontz, Ph.D., and guitarists Troy King, Brian Kooken and Vincent Corson.

Brian has performed jazz, popular, and classical genres at Maryland music venues (see list of performances). One noteworthy recent performance was PRISM: A musical Collage at Kaplan Concert Hall. This fast-paced program featured virtually every ensemble and area in the Department of Music in one continuous performance with dramatic lighting. Brian has also performed in masterclasses with world-renowned guitarists such as Meng Su and Irina Kulikova. Some noteworthy masterclasses Brian has performed in were with Meng Su and Irina Kulikova. Brian has been a guitarist in the band Intuitive Minds United for over a decade.

Brian also has a home recording studio and has produced over a hundred music-related videos, which have garnered hundreds of thousands of views. Mr. Streckfus prides himself on his audio, video, and photo editing. Brian's extensive formal training in combination with an undogmatic, best-of-both-worlds jazz and classical approach, diverse repertoire, and music technology skills make him an excellent music instructor and performer.

Media

Here are just some of the features of my music arrangements and music method book:

  1. Playable music theory
  2. Chord names and diagrams
  3. Letter names to note heads to aid in learning to sight-read
  4. Fingerings to remove the tedium
  5. Bold fonts that give me arrangements more of a jazz lead sheet style
  6. Pieces that were originally for an ensemble arranged to solo guitar
  7. Guitar tablature
  8. Beautifully engraved sections so that that phrasing is obvious

Publications are on sheetmusicdirect.com, sheetmusicplus.com, and musescore.org. They are designed so that a hobbyist can successfully play the music with clarity in a short amount of time, but my materials are also sophisticated enough so that a professional can dive deeply into analysis. Mr. Streckfus believes this is the perfect fit for his skills, and that it is an untapped aspect of the music industry; there are millions of hobbyist guitarists that are learning music in an inefficient way and these publications aim to fix that problem.
